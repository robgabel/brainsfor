**Melinda Ann French Gates**[\[2\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melinda_French_Gates#cite_note-:6-2) (née **French**; born August 15, 1964) is an American [philanthropist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philanthropy "Philanthropy"). Born and raised in [Dallas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dallas "Dallas"), Texas, she attended [Duke University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duke_University "Duke University"), where she earned a bachelor's degree in computer science and economics and an MBA. She joined [Microsoft](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microsoft "Microsoft") in 1987 as a multimedia product developer. In the same year, she began dating the company's co-founder and then-chief executive [Bill Gates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bill_Gates "Bill Gates"), whom she married in 1994 and divorced in 2021. They have three children together.

From 2000 to 2024, she and Gates co-chaired the [Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bill_&_Melinda_Gates_Foundation "Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation"), the world's largest private charitable organization.[\[3\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melinda_French_Gates#cite_note-3) For their philanthropic endeavors, which focused on global health, development, and education, they received numerous awards and honors, including the U.S. [Presidential Medal of Freedom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Presidential_Medal_of_Freedom "Presidential Medal of Freedom") and the French [Legion of Honour](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legion_of_Honour "Legion of Honour"). After French Gates resigned as co-chair in 2024, Gates became sole chair of the renamed Gates Foundation.[\[4\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melinda_French_Gates#cite_note-:8-4)

As part of her divorce settlement, French Gates received $12.5 billion for independent philanthropy, which she has stated will focus on women and families.[\[5\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melinda_French_Gates#cite_note-5)[\[6\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melinda_French_Gates#cite_note-6) She has become a prominent [megadonor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Megadonor "Megadonor") to the [Democratic Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States) "Democratic Party (United States)"), with a focus on [abortion rights](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abortion_in_the_United_States "Abortion in the United States"), and endorsed [Kamala Harris](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kamala_Harris "Kamala Harris") in the [2024 US presidential election](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2024_United_States_presidential_election "2024 United States presidential election").[\[7\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melinda_French_Gates#cite_note-7) In 2025, she published a memoir, _The Next Day: Transition, Change and Moving Forward_.[\[8\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melinda_French_Gates#cite_note-8)

Consistently ranked by _[Forbes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forbes "Forbes")_ magazine as one of the world's most powerful women,[\[9\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melinda_French_Gates#cite_note-The_World-9) French Gates was recognized as one of the [BBC's 100 women of 2021](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/100_Women_(BBC) "100 Women (BBC)").[\[10\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melinda_French_Gates#cite_note-:3-10) As of February 2026, her net worth is estimated at US$30.4 billion, making her the world's 72nd wealthiest person, according to _Forbes_.[\[11\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melinda_French_Gates#cite_note-f2025-11)

## Early life and education

Melinda Ann French was born on August 15, 1964, in [Dallas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dallas "Dallas"), Texas.[\[12\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melinda_French_Gates#cite_note-:0-12)[\[13\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melinda_French_Gates#cite_note-:7-13)[\[14\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melinda_French_Gates#cite_note-14) She is the second of four children born to Raymond Joseph French Jr., an [aerospace engineer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aerospace_engineer "Aerospace engineer"), and Elaine Agnes Amerland, a [homemaker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Homemaker "Homemaker"). She has an older sister and two younger brothers.[\[15\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melinda_French_Gates#cite_note-15)

French Gates, a [Catholic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catholic "Catholic"), attended St. Monica Catholic School, where she was the [valedictorian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Valedictorian "Valedictorian") of her class.[\[16\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melinda_French_Gates#cite_note-Catholic_2-16)[\[17\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melinda_French_Gates#cite_note-Catholic_1-17) At age 14, French Gates was introduced to the [Apple II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_II "Apple II") by her father and Mrs. Bauer, a school teacher who advocated teaching computer science at the all-girls school.[\[18\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melinda_French_Gates#cite_note-18) It was from this experience she developed her interest in computer games and the [BASIC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BASIC "BASIC") programming language.[\[19\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melinda_French_Gates#cite_note-19)

French Gates graduated as valedictorian from [Ursuline Academy of Dallas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ursuline_Academy_of_Dallas "Ursuline Academy of Dallas") in 1982.[\[20\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melinda_French_Gates#cite_note-20) She earned a [bachelor's degree](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bachelor's_degree "Bachelor's degree") in [computer science](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_science "Computer science") and [economics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economics "Economics") from [Duke University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duke_University "Duke University") in 1986 and an [MBA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Master_of_Business_Administration "Master of Business Administration") from Duke's [Fuqua School of Business](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fuqua_School_of_Business "Fuqua School of Business") in 1987.[\[21\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melinda_French_Gates#cite_note-StangeOyster2013-21) At Duke, French Gates was a member of the [Kappa Alpha Theta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kappa_Alpha_Theta "Kappa Alpha Theta") sorority, Beta Rho Chapter.[\[22\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melinda_French_Gates#cite_note-22)

### Early career and Microsoft

French Gates's first job was tutoring children in [mathematics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematics "Mathematics") and [computer programming](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_programming "Computer programming").[\[23\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melinda_French_Gates#cite_note-23) After graduation, she became a marketing manager with Microsoft, being responsible for the development of multimedia products.[\[24\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melinda_French_Gates#cite_note-:1-24) These included Microsoft [Cinemania](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microsoft_Cinemania "Microsoft Cinemania"), [Encarta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Encarta "Encarta"), [Publisher](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microsoft_Publisher "Microsoft Publisher"), [Bob](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microsoft_Bob "Microsoft Bob"), [Money](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microsoft_Money "Microsoft Money"), [Works](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microsoft_Works "Microsoft Works") and [Word](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microsoft_Word "Microsoft Word").[\[24\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melinda_French_Gates#cite_note-:1-24)[\[25\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melinda_French_Gates#cite_note-25) She worked on [Expedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Expedia "Expedia"), which became one of the most popular travel booking websites. In the early 1990s, French Gates was appointed as [general manager](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_manager "General manager") of Information Products, a position which she held until 1996.[\[26\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melinda_French_Gates#cite_note-:2-26)[\[27\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melinda_French_Gates#cite_note-Melinda_Gates_Biography-27) She left Microsoft that year, reportedly, to focus on starting a family.[\[26\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melinda_French_Gates#cite_note-:2-26)

### Corporate directorship

French Gates served as a member of Duke University's board of trustees from 1996 to 2003.[\[28\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melinda_French_Gates#cite_note-28) She attends the annual [Bilderberg Group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bilderberg_Group "Bilderberg Group") conference and has held a seat on the board of directors of [Graham Holdings](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Graham_Holdings "Graham Holdings") (formerly [The Washington Post](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Washington_Post "The Washington Post") Company) since 2004.[\[29\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melinda_French_Gates#cite_note-29) She was also on the board of directors at [Drugstore.com](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drugstore.com "Drugstore.com") but left in August 2006 to focus on philanthropic projects.[\[30\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melinda_French_Gates#cite_note-30)[\[27\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melinda_French_Gates#cite_note-Melinda_Gates_Biography-27)
