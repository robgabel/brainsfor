**Anne LaBarr Duke** (née **Lederer**; born September 13, 1965) is an American former professional [poker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poker "Poker") player and author in cognitive-behavioral decision science and decision education. She holds a [World Series of Poker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Series_of_Poker "World Series of Poker") (WSOP) gold bracelet from 2004 and used to be the leading money winner among women in WSOP history; she remains in the top five as of April 2023, despite having retired from poker and last cashing at a tournament in 2010.[\[2\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annie_Duke#cite_note-WATML-2) Duke won the 2004 [World Series of Poker Tournament of Champions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Series_of_Poker_Tournament_of_Champions "World Series of Poker Tournament of Champions") and the [National Heads-Up Poker Championship](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Heads-Up_Poker_Championship "National Heads-Up Poker Championship") in 2010. She has written a number of instructional books for poker players, including _Decide to Play Great Poker_ and _The Middle Zone_, as well as an autobiography, _How I Raised, Folded, Bluffed, Flirted, Cursed, and Won Millions at the World Series of Poker_, in 2005. Duke authored two books on decision-making,[\[3\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annie_Duke#cite_note-3) _Thinking in Bets: Making Smarter Decisions When You Don't Have All the Facts_, and _How to Decide: Simple Tools for Making Better Choices_.

Duke co-founded the non-profit [Ante Up for Africa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-bracelet_events_at_the_WSOP#Ante_Up_for_Africa "Non-bracelet events at the WSOP") with actor [Don Cheadle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Don_Cheadle "Don Cheadle") in 2007 to benefit charities working in African nations, and has raised money for other charities and non-profits through playing in and hosting charitable poker tournaments. She has been involved in advocacy on a number of poker-related issues including advocating for the legality of online gambling and for players' rights to control their own image. Duke was co-founder, executive vice president, and commissioner of the [Epic Poker League](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Epic_Poker_League "Epic Poker League") from 2011 to 2012.[\[4\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annie_Duke#cite_note-4)

## Early life and family

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Annie_Duke&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Early life and family")\]

Duke, born as Anne LaBarr Lederer,[\[5\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annie_Duke#cite_note-NYTStyle-5) grew up in [Concord, New Hampshire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Concord,_New_Hampshire "Concord, New Hampshire"), where her father, writer and linguist [Richard Lederer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_Lederer_(writer) "Richard Lederer (writer)"), taught English literature at [St. Paul's School](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/St._Paul%27s_School_(Concord,_New_Hampshire) "St. Paul's School (Concord, New Hampshire)")[\[1\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annie_Duke#cite_note-Cheney-1) and her mother, Rhoda S. Lederer, daughter of trial attorney [Craig Spangenberg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Craig_Spangenberg "Craig Spangenberg"),[\[5\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annie_Duke#cite_note-NYTStyle-5) taught at Concord High School.[\[5\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annie_Duke#cite_note-NYTStyle-5)[\[6\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annie_Duke#cite_note-Jones-6) Her parents were both card players[\[7\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annie_Duke#cite_note-Friess-7) and Duke became interested in cards from an early age.[\[8\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annie_Duke#cite_note-Deitsch-8) Her siblings are professional poker player [Howard Lederer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Howard_Lederer "Howard Lederer") and author/poet [Katy Lederer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Katy_Lederer "Katy Lederer"), who published a memoir about the Lederer family.[\[9\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annie_Duke#cite_note-Sauer-9)

Duke attended St. Paul's School.[\[5\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annie_Duke#cite_note-NYTStyle-5) While a student there, she worked at [Kentucky Fried Chicken](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kentucky_Fried_Chicken "Kentucky Fried Chicken"). She enrolled at [Columbia University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Columbia_University "Columbia University"), joining the first [co-ed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Co-Ed "Co-Ed") class in its 230-year history, and pursued a double major in [English](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/English_studies "English studies") and [psychology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Psychology "Psychology").[\[1\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annie_Duke#cite_note-Cheney-1)[\[10\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annie_Duke#cite_note-10)[\[11\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annie_Duke#cite_note-11) After graduating from Columbia, she pursued a [Ph.D.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doctor_of_Philosophy "Doctor of Philosophy") in psychology at the [University of Pennsylvania](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Pennsylvania "University of Pennsylvania"), focusing on [cognitive linguistics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cognitive_linguistics "Cognitive linguistics") and writing her [dissertation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thesis "Thesis") on the hypothesis of [syntactic bootstrapping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syntactic_bootstrapping "Syntactic bootstrapping").[\[12\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annie_Duke#cite_note-Bellafante-12) For her graduate studies she was awarded a [National Science Foundation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Science_Foundation "National Science Foundation") fellowship.[\[1\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annie_Duke#cite_note-Cheney-1) In 1991, one month before [defending](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dissertation#North_America "Dissertation") her doctoral dissertation, she decided that she no longer wished to pursue a career in academia and left school.[\[6\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annie_Duke#cite_note-Jones-6)

In 1992, she married Ben Duke, grandson of Ambassador [Angier Biddle Duke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Angier_Biddle_Duke "Angier Biddle Duke") and a descendent of [Washington Duke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Washington_Duke "Washington Duke"), and moved to [Billings, Montana](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Billings,_Montana "Billings, Montana").[\[5\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annie_Duke#cite_note-NYTStyle-5)[\[13\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annie_Duke#cite_note-Darrow-13) The couple divided their time between [Las Vegas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Las_Vegas_Valley "Las Vegas Valley") and Montana between 1992 and 2002, when they moved to [Portland, Oregon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portland,_Oregon "Portland, Oregon"). They were married until 2004 and had four children.[\[12\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annie_Duke#cite_note-Bellafante-12)[\[14\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annie_Duke#cite_note-Berger-14) Maud Duke was born in 1995; Leo Duke, in 1998; Lucy Duke, in 2000; and Nell Duke, in 2002. In 2005, Duke and her children relocated to [Hollywood Hills, California](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hollywood_Hills,_California "Hollywood Hills, California").[\[1\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annie_Duke#cite_note-Cheney-1)[\[12\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annie_Duke#cite_note-Bellafante-12) She later married Eric Brooks, cofounder of [Susquehanna International Group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Susquehanna_International_Group "Susquehanna International Group").[\[15\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annie_Duke#cite_note-15)

More than thirty years after leaving graduate school, she returned to the University of Pennsylvania in the fall of 2022, earning her Ph.D. in cognitive psychology in June 2023.[\[16\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annie_Duke#cite_note-v823-16)[\[17\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annie_Duke#cite_note-17) She now serves as an adjunct professor with Harvard Kennedy School and UPenn Wharton School of Executive Education.[\[18\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annie_Duke#cite_note-18)[\[19\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annie_Duke#cite_note-19)

## Professional poker career

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Annie_Duke&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Professional poker career")\]

### Early career

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Annie_Duke&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Early career")\]

Duke first played [Texas hold'em](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Texas_hold%27em "Texas hold'em") at age twenty-two in a casino and continued to play for fun in Las Vegas casinos while visiting her brother [Howard Lederer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Howard_Lederer "Howard Lederer") during her graduate school years.[\[1\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annie_Duke#cite_note-Cheney-1) In 1992, after Duke moved to Billings, her brother encouraged her to play poker professionally, sending her $2,400 and providing her with poker instruction books and lessons by phone.[\[8\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annie_Duke#cite_note-Deitsch-8) She began to play poker initially at the Crystal Lounge, a local bar in Billings that had a legal poker room. Following a successful year playing in Montana, her brother prompted her to enter tournaments at the [1994 World Series of Poker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1994_World_Series_of_Poker "1994 World Series of Poker") (WSOP) in Las Vegas.[\[1\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annie_Duke#cite_note-Cheney-1) Within the first month, she won $70,000 and decided to move to Las Vegas to pursue a professional poker career.[\[6\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annie_Duke#cite_note-Jones-6)

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Annie_Duke&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Live poker")\]
