![](https://qph.cf2.quoracdn.net/main-qimg-d7f3e3b55a0b39f0a38ece1340de6674.webp)

The Omni solves this problem by introducing locomotion into the VR experience so that you can roam around unbounded in your VR world.

Other Top benefits:

- Viewing and moving are bifurcated. You can look in one direction while moving in the opposite direction.
- No motion sickness. Since your body is actually doing what your brain is perceiving, you won't get sick
- Unlimited movement. You're not bounded by the size of the physical room you're in. You can run for 20 miles on the Omni! (No bumping into your coffee table!)
- You can walk, run, sit, jump and crouch in the Omni (while still using your tool/brush/gun/laser/shooter)
- The Omni is small \- only approx 3.5 or 4 feet in diameter.
- Great way to actually experience and walk-through new places or spaces
- Great for exercise! Couch potatoes rejoice!
- Walking/running is natural, safe and easy! Anyone can use it, easily.

05. Simulations & Simulators of any kind (Hazmat Suits, Space-Suits, Skydiving or Wing-Suits,
06. Exo-Skeletons, or using / testing-out new tools & equipment
07. Army, Military, Armed-Services & other Defense applications (training for mountain, desert, space or swamp-like battle environments)

Remember, Facebook NEVER buys a company for the company itself or the product it is currently building.

Facebook buys a company for EXCELLENT PEOPLE with unique and awesome technical expertise.

This is stated flatly by Zuck himself, as reported by TechCrunch: http://techcrunch.com/2010/11/09/arms-race/

And I quote: "Mark Zuckerberg even has put the acqui-hire equivalent of a want ad on Quora, asking for cool suggestions for companies to acquire. Word around the startup world is that “Why not pay $1 million to hire a few good engineers?” isn’t an irrational question. Zuckerberg himself has said, “Facebook has not once bought a company for the company itself. We buy companies to get excellent people.”"

Now, several companies that are replete with excellent technical people -- Asana, Quora, Path, and Square -- are not possible for Facebook to acquire at this time due to structural reasons.

A few other companies that are replete with excellent technical people -- Posterous, Alphonso Labs (makers of Pulse), Campfire Labs, MyLikes, Thingd, SimpleGeo, Instagram, and Apture -- just plain are not for sale any time soon. Too bad for Facebook.

Facebook has a great track record so far -- nothing but excellent acquisitions -- and they really should not lower their standards.

The thing is, there are only a few truly excellent companies out there, because the marketplace is so competitive now, and Google has eyes and ears everywhere.
