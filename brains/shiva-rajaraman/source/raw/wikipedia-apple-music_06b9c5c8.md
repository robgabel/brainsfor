Apple Music

**Apple Music** is a [music streaming service](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Music_streaming_service "Music streaming service") launched by [Apple](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Inc. "Apple Inc.") in June 2015. The service is available in 167 countries and currently has over 150 million songs.[\[5\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Music#cite_note-7) Apple Music runs the free [internet radio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internet_radio "Internet radio") stations [Apple Music 1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Music_1 "Apple Music 1"), Apple Music Hits, Apple Music Country, Apple Música Uno, Apple Music Club, and Apple Music Chill which are [broadcast](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Broadcasting "Broadcasting") live 24 hours a day.[\[6\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Music#cite_note-:2-8)[\[7\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Music#cite_note-9)

Originally strictly a music service, Apple Music began expanding into video in 2016. Then-executive [Jimmy Iovine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jimmy_Iovine "Jimmy Iovine") stated that the intention for the service is to become a "cultural platform", and Apple reportedly wants the service to be a "one-stop shop for pop culture". The company is actively investing heavily in the production and purchasing of video content, both in terms of music videos and concert footage that support music releases, as well as web series and feature films.\[ _[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_\]

Apple Music gained popularity rapidly after its launch, reaching 10 million subscribers in six months.\[ _[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_\]

As of May 2023[\[update\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Apple_Music&action=edit), the most streamed song of all time on Apple Music is " [Shape of You](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shape_of_You "Shape of You")" by [Ed Sheeran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ed_Sheeran "Ed Sheeran") with more than 930 million plays worldwide.[\[8\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Music#cite_note-10)

## Features

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Apple_Music&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Features")\]

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/c/c5/Apple_Music_for_Android_screenshot.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Apple_Music_for_Android_screenshot.png) Apple Music home screen for Android

Apple Music subscribers can create a profile to share their music with friends and follow other users to view the music they are listening to on a regular basis.[\[9\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Music#cite_note-:3-11)[\[10\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Music#cite_note-12) Apple Music's use of [iCloud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ICloud "ICloud"), which matches a users' songs to those found on the service, allows users to combine their iTunes music library with their Apple Music library and listen to their music all in one place.

In 2018, with the release of iOS 12, Apple Music added the ability to search for a song by its lyrics.[\[11\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Music#cite_note-:4-13)

Users also have the ability to view their most played songs, artists, and albums of the entire year through a feature called Apple Music replay.[\[12\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Music#cite_note-auto-14)[\[13\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Music#cite_note-15) This feature tracks listening times down to the minute giving users accurate information on how much they may have listened to a specific song, artist, or album. The feature also tells the user specific genres that they listened to throughout the year, placing them in order from most listened-to least listened-to. Apple Music Replay also provides an interactive system where it plays a generated animation that recaps what the user's activity was like over the past year, along with a milestone section that shows specific goals they reached.[\[12\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Music#cite_note-auto-14)

### Pre-launch

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Apple_Music&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Pre-launch")\]

Before Apple Music, the company's [iPod](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IPod "IPod") and [iTunes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ITunes "ITunes") were known for having "revolutionized digital music."[\[14\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Music#cite_note-Announcement_by_The_Verge-16) Former Apple CEO [Steve Jobs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steve_Jobs "Steve Jobs") was known to be opposed to the idea of music subscription services.[\[15\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Music#cite_note-17) When Apple bought audio equipment maker [Beats Electronics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beats_Electronics "Beats Electronics") in 2014, Apple gained ownership of Beats' own service [Beats Music](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beats_Music "Beats Music"),[\[16\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Music#cite_note-18) and made Beats Music CEO Ian Rogers responsible for the [iTunes Radio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ITunes_Radio "ITunes Radio") service.[\[17\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Music#cite_note-19) _[Business Insider](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Business_Insider "Business Insider")_ later reported that Apple was planning to merge the two services. Apple also hired noted [New Zealand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Zealand "New Zealand") born British radio DJ [Zane Lowe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zane_Lowe "Zane Lowe") to serve as a music curator.[\[18\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Music#cite_note-20)

Shortly before Apple Music was released, singer-songwriter [Taylor Swift](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taylor_Swift "Taylor Swift") wrote an open letter publicly criticizing Apple's decision to not reimburse artists during a user's free trial period and announced that she would be holding back her album _[1989](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1989_(Taylor_Swift_album) "1989 (Taylor Swift album)")_ from the service. She said the policy was "unfair" as "Apple Music will not be paying writers, producers, or artists for those months".[\[19\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Music#cite_note-21)[\[20\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Music#cite_note-22) UK independent record label [Beggars Group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beggars_Group "Beggars Group") also criticized the trial period, saying it struggled "to see why rights owners and artists should bear this aspect of Apple's customer acquisition costs".[\[21\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Music#cite_note-23)[\[22\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Music#cite_note-24)
