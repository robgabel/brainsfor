**William Henry Green II** (born May 5, 1980) is an American [YouTuber](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/YouTuber "YouTuber"), [science communicator](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Science_communicator "Science communicator"), novelist, [stand-up comedian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stand-up_comedy "Stand-up comedy"), and entrepreneur. He produces the [YouTube](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/YouTube "YouTube") channel [Vlogbrothers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vlogbrothers "Vlogbrothers") with his older brother, author [John Green](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Green "John Green"), and hosts the educational YouTube channels [_Crash Course_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crash_Course_(YouTube) "Crash Course (YouTube)") and _[SciShow](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SciShow "SciShow")_. He has advocated for and organized [social activism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_activism "Social activism"), created and hosted a number of other YouTube channels and podcasts, released music albums, and amassed a large following on [TikTok](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/TikTok "TikTok").

[Hank and his brother John](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Green_brothers "Green brothers") co-created [VidCon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/VidCon "VidCon"), the world's largest conference about online videos, and the [Project for Awesome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Project_for_Awesome "Project for Awesome"), an annual online charity event, as well as the now-defunct conference NerdCon: Stories, focused on storytelling. He is the co-creator of _[The Lizzie Bennet Diaries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Lizzie_Bennet_Diaries "The Lizzie Bennet Diaries")_ (2012–2013), an adaptation of _[Pride and Prejudice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pride_and_Prejudice "Pride and Prejudice")_ in the style of video blogs that was the first web series to win an [Emmy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emmy_Award "Emmy Award"). He is also the co-founder of merchandise company [DFTBA Records](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DFTBA_Records "DFTBA Records"), crowdfunding platform Subbable (acquired by [Patreon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Patreon "Patreon")), game company DFTBA Games, and online video production company Pemberley Digital, which produces video blog adaptations of classic novels in the public domain. Green is the founder of the environmental technology blog EcoGeek, which evolved into [Complexly](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Complexly "Complexly"), an online video and audio production company of which he was the CEO until late 2023. Green also hosts the podcasts _[Dear Hank & John](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dear_Hank_&_John "Dear Hank & John")_ and _Delete This_ with his brother and wife respectively, along with the podcast _[SciShow Tangents](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SciShow_Tangents "SciShow Tangents")_.

Green's [debut novel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Debut_novel "Debut novel"), _[An Absolutely Remarkable Thing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/An_Absolutely_Remarkable_Thing "An Absolutely Remarkable Thing")_, was published on September 25, 2018; its sequel _[A Beautifully Foolish Endeavor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A_Beautifully_Foolish_Endeavor "A Beautifully Foolish Endeavor")_ was published on July 7, 2020. Both novels debuted as [_New York Times_ Best Sellers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_Times_Best_Seller "New York Times Best Seller"). In response to being diagnosed and treated for [Hodgkin lymphoma](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hodgkin_lymphoma "Hodgkin lymphoma") in 2023, Green stepped down as CEO of his companies. While recovering, Green began performing [stand-up](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stand-up "Stand-up") about his experience. His [comedy special](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comedy_special "Comedy special") titled _Pissing Out Cancer_ was released on the streaming service [Dropout](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dropout_(media_company) "Dropout (media company)") on June 21, 2024. In July 2025, Green partnered with Honey B Games to launch Focus Friend, a productivity app which allows users to set a timer that temporarily blocks other apps. The app reached number one on [Apple's App Store](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/App_Store_(Apple) "App Store (Apple)") charts for free apps.

## Early life and career

William Henry Green II was born on May 5, 1980, to Mike and Sydney Green[\[1\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hank_Green#cite_note-1)[\[2\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hank_Green#cite_note-2) in [Birmingham, Alabama](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Birmingham,_Alabama "Birmingham, Alabama"),[\[3\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hank_Green#cite_note-may16-3) but his family soon moved to [Orlando](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orlando,_Florida "Orlando, Florida"), [Florida](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Florida "Florida"), where he was raised.[\[3\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hank_Green#cite_note-may16-3) Green is of [Irish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Irish_Americans "Irish Americans") ancestry.[\[4\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hank_Green#cite_note-4) He graduated from [Winter Park High School](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Winter_Park_High_School "Winter Park High School") in 1998. He then earned a [B.S.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bachelor_of_Science "Bachelor of Science") in [biochemistry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biochemistry "Biochemistry") from [Eckerd College](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eckerd_College "Eckerd College") in 2002 and a [M.S.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Master_of_Science "Master of Science") in [environmental studies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Environmental_studies "Environmental studies") from the [University of Montana](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Montana "University of Montana").[\[5\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hank_Green#cite_note-myspace-5) His master's thesis was titled "Of Both Worlds: How the Personal Computer and the Environmental Movement Change Everything".[\[6\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hank_Green#cite_note-6)

Throughout high school and college, Green created and designed websites for himself and local clients. His first project, the "Mars Exploration Page", in 1994, experienced minor success on the heels of the [Mars Pathfinder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mars_Pathfinder "Mars Pathfinder") Mission. A later website, IHateI4.com, which was about Green's dislike for [Interstate 4](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interstate_4 "Interstate 4"), a widely unpopular highway among Floridians, brought press from local news channels and the _[Orlando Sentinel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orlando_Sentinel "Orlando Sentinel")_.[\[7\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hank_Green#cite_note-ihateiE-7) Green continued as a [web developer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Web_developer "Web developer") after moving to [Montana](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Montana "Montana") for [graduate school](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Graduate_school "Graduate school"), focusing on developing websites for educational institutions (including the [University of Montana](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Montana "University of Montana")) and environmental [nonprofit organizations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nonprofit_organization "Nonprofit organization").

While in graduate school, Green created "EcoGeek", a blog focusing on technological advancements that benefit the environment.[\[8\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hank_Green#cite_note-visionary-8) Starting as a class project,[\[9\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hank_Green#cite_note-9) EcoGeek evolved into a major environmental publication.[\[10\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hank_Green#cite_note-10) It caught the attention of _[Time](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time_(magazine) "Time (magazine)")_, where it was described as "porn for hardcore science, tech and enviro freaks".[\[11\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hank_Green#cite_note-11) Writing about environmental issues, Green has been published on numerous environmental blogs, including Treehugger.com, Yahoo! Green, _[The National Geographic Green Guide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Green_Guide "The Green Guide")_, _[Scientific American](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scientific_American "Scientific American")_, [The Weather Channel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Weather_Channel "The Weather Channel"), [Planet Green](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planet_Green "Planet Green"), [NPR](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Public_Radio "National Public Radio"),[\[12\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hank_Green#cite_note-12) and in _[The New York Times](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_New_York_Times "The New York Times")_.[\[13\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hank_Green#cite_note-13) During the mid-2000s, Green wrote regularly for _[Mental Floss](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mental_Floss "Mental Floss")_ and co-authored one of their books, _Mental Floss: Scatterbrained_.[\[14\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hank_Green#cite_note-14)[\[15\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hank_Green#cite_note-Ventura2009MF-15)
