**Peter Attia** (born March 19, 1973)[\[1\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Attia#cite_note-amazon1-1) is a Canadian-American author and former researcher known for his work in [longevity medicine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Age_management_medicine "Age management medicine"). He is the author of _[Outlive: The Science and Art of Longevity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Outlive:_The_Science_and_Art_of_Longevity "Outlive: The Science and Art of Longevity")._

## Early life and education

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Peter_Attia&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Early life and education")\]

Attia was born and raised in [Toronto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toronto "Toronto"), Ontario, Canada. He is the child of Egyptian Christian immigrant parents.[\[2\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Attia#cite_note-Machell2023-2) He graduated from [Queen's University at Kingston](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Queen%27s_University_at_Kingston "Queen's University at Kingston") in 1996 with a Bachelor of Science in mechanical engineering and [applied mathematics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Applied_mathematics "Applied mathematics"). He then attended [Stanford University School of Medicine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stanford_University_School_of_Medicine "Stanford University School of Medicine"), graduating in 2001 with a [Doctor of Medicine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doctor_of_Medicine "Doctor of Medicine").\[ _[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_\]

## Career

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Peter_Attia&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Career")\]

From 2001 to 2006, Attia began a residency in [general surgery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_surgery "General surgery") at the [Johns Hopkins Hospital](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johns_Hopkins_Hospital "Johns Hopkins Hospital") in [Baltimore](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baltimore "Baltimore"), Maryland, but never completed his residency, nor did he complete a fellowship or become board certified.[\[3\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Attia#cite_note-3) He also undertook research at the [NIH](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NIH "NIH") in [Bethesda, Maryland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bethesda,_Maryland "Bethesda, Maryland"), investigating [cancer immunotherapy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cancer_immunotherapy "Cancer immunotherapy") for [melanoma](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melanoma "Melanoma").[\[4\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Attia#cite_note-4)

After dropping out of his residency program, Attia joined the consulting firm [McKinsey & Company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/McKinsey_%26_Company "McKinsey & Company") in the [Palo Alto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palo_Alto,_California "Palo Alto, California") office as a member of the Corporate Risk Practice and Healthcare Practice.[\[5\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Attia#cite_note-5) In 2014, he founded a private clinic dedicated to [longevity medicine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Longevity_medicine "Longevity medicine").[\[6\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Attia#cite_note-6) Attia also created the blog "The Eating Academy" (later "War on Insulin" and now peterattiamd.com) that mostly focuses on topics related to [nutrition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nutrition "Nutrition"), [physical activity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Physical_activity "Physical activity"), and [longevity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Longevity "Longevity"). Subsequently, he launched the podcast _The Peter Attia Drive_, in which he interviews experts each week, covering topics such as longevity, metabolic health, and medical research.

In 2012, Attia co-founded the Nutrition Science Initiative (NuSI) with [Gary Taubes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gary_Taubes "Gary Taubes"), with a primary focus on promoting nutrition research and tackling the growing health challenges linked to obesity, diabetes, and metabolic diseases.[\[7\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Attia#cite_note-7)[\[8\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Attia#cite_note-8) In 2013, Attia was a speaker at [TEDMED](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/TEDMED "TEDMED"), where he shared insights on longevity.[\[9\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Attia#cite_note-Oconnor2013-9)[\[10\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Attia#cite_note-10)[\[11\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Attia#cite_note-11) Attia has a chapter giving advice in [Tim Ferriss](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tim_Ferriss "Tim Ferriss")'s book _[Tools of Titans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tools_of_Titans "Tools of Titans")_. He had a central role in _[Limitless with Chris Hemsworth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Limitless_with_Chris_Hemsworth "Limitless with Chris Hemsworth")_, a six-part documentary for [National Geographic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Geographic "National Geographic") and [Disney+](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disney%2B "Disney+") starring [Chris Hemsworth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chris_Hemsworth "Chris Hemsworth") and directed by [Darren Aronofsky](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Darren_Aronofsky "Darren Aronofsky"). In episode 5, Attia told Hemsworth that he carried the high-risk [APOE4](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/APOE4 "APOE4") allele, which places him at elevated risk of [neurodegenerative aging of the Alzheimer's type](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alzheimer%27s_disease "Alzheimer's disease").[\[12\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Attia#cite_note-GMA2022-12)

In 2017, he posted on his YouTube Channel, with his first set of videos being instructional exercise videos. It now has more than a million subscribers and focuses on longevity content.[\[13\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Attia#cite_note-13)

On March 28, 2023, Attia (with coauthor Bill Gifford) published his book [_Outlive: The Science & Art of Longevity_.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Outlive:_The_Science_and_Art_of_Longevity "Outlive: The Science and Art of Longevity")[\[14\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Attia#cite_note-14) According to _[The New York Times Magazine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_New_York_Times_Magazine "The New York Times Magazine")_, the book "has been a runaway best seller since it was published this spring \[of 2023\]";[\[15\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Attia#cite_note-Marchese2023-15) it was No. 3 on the Amazon Charts for nonfiction as of August 24, 2023.[\[16\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Attia#cite_note-AmazonCharts-16)[\[15\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Attia#cite_note-Marchese2023-15) Attia was named in _Time_'s 2024 list of influential people in health. [\[17\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Attia#cite_note-17)

In October 2025, Attia appeared on CBS's _60 Minutes_, where he discussed the role of structured exercise and preventative health strategies in extending health span.[\[18\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Attia#cite_note-18)

In January 2026, Attia, along with various other health influencers, was announced as a new contributor to [CBS News](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CBS_News "CBS News").[\[19\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Attia#cite_note-19) Attia resigned the following month after the release of the [Epstein files](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Epstein_files "Epstein files") revealed that he had exchanged emails with child sex offender [Jeffrey Epstein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jeffrey_Epstein "Jeffrey Epstein").[\[20\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Attia#cite_note-20)

## Relationship with Jeffrey Epstein

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Peter_Attia&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Relationship with Jeffrey Epstein")\]

In 2015, Attia met with Epstein through [Eva Andersson-Dubin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eva_Andersson-Dubin "Eva Andersson-Dubin"). "He might literally be one of the—if not the—most interesting people I've ever met!" Attia appears to have emailed Andersson-Dubin in June 2015. Andersson-Dubin forwarded that email to Epstein.[\[21\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Attia#cite_note-PoliticoArticleMarch2026-21)

In 2025, the disclosure of Jeffrey Epstein's appointment calendars revealed several meetings with Attia up to 2019.[\[22\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Attia#cite_note-22) When the Epstein files were released, emails between Attia and Epstein attested to their longstanding friendship; in one of them, dated June 2015, Epstein wrote to Attia, with the subject header "Got a fresh shipment". Attia told Epstein that "the biggest problem" of being friends with him was that "the life you lead is so outrageous, and yet I can't tell a soul".[\[23\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Attia#cite_note-23) In a separate email dated February 2016, he told Epstein that "pussy is, indeed, low carb."[\[24\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Attia#cite_note-24)[\[25\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Attia#cite_note-25)[\[26\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Attia#cite_note-26) Emails show Attia asked Epstein for patient referrals in 2015, stayed in an empty Upper East Side apartment Epstein owned in 2016, discussed the 2017 job opening with Epstein for Head of Patient Experience he was hiring and, over several years, provided him with health advice and testing.[\[21\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Attia#cite_note-PoliticoArticleMarch2026-21) The emails revealed Attia told Epstein in February 2016 he would love to add "5 years or more to your life … even if the only reason to do so is to have more sex." In another exchange several days later, Attia asked Epstein: "Have you decided if you're interested in living longer (solely for the ladies, of course)?"[\[21\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Attia#cite_note-PoliticoArticleMarch2026-21)

Following the revelations of Attia's relationship with Epstein, in February 2026, Attia resigned his position as contributor to [CBS News](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CBS_News "CBS News"). Attia also resigned from his position as Chief Science Officer at David, a protein bar company.[\[27\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Attia#cite_note-27)
