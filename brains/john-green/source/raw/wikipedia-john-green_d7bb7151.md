**John Michael Green** (born August 24, 1977) is an American author and [YouTuber](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/YouTuber "YouTuber"). His books have more than 50million copies in print worldwide, including _[The Fault in Our Stars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Fault_in_Our_Stars "The Fault in Our Stars")_ (2012), which is one of the [best-selling books of all time](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_best-selling_books#Between_20_million_and_50_million_copies "List of best-selling books"). Green's rapid rise to fame and idiosyncratic voice are credited with creating a major shift in the [young adult fiction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Young_adult_fiction "Young adult fiction") market. Green is also well known for his work in online video, most notably his YouTube ventures with his younger brother [Hank Green](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hank_Green "Hank Green").

Born in [Indianapolis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indianapolis "Indianapolis"), Indiana, Green was raised in [Orlando, Florida](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orlando,_Florida "Orlando, Florida"), before attending boarding school outside of [Birmingham, Alabama](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Birmingham,_Alabama "Birmingham, Alabama"). He attended [Kenyon College](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kenyon_College "Kenyon College"), graduating with a double major in [English](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/English_studies "English studies") and [religious studies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Religious_studies "Religious studies") in 2000. Green then spent six months as a student chaplain at a children's hospital. He reconsidered his path and began working at _[Booklist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Booklist "Booklist")_ in Chicago while writing his first novel. His debut novel _[Looking for Alaska](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Looking_for_Alaska "Looking for Alaska")_ (2005) was awarded the 2006 [Michael L. Printz Award](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michael_L._Printz_Award "Michael L. Printz Award"). While living in New York City, Green published his second novel, _[An Abundance of Katherines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/An_Abundance_of_Katherines "An Abundance of Katherines")_ (2006). Starting on January 1, 2007, [John and his brother Hank](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Green_brothers "Green brothers") launched the [Vlogbrothers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vlogbrothers "Vlogbrothers") YouTube channel, a series of [vlogs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vlog "Vlog") submitted to one another on alternating weekdays; the videos spawned an active online-based community called [Nerdfighteria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nerdfighteria "Nerdfighteria") and an annual [telethon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Telethon "Telethon")-style fundraiser called [Project for Awesome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Project_for_Awesome "Project for Awesome"), both of which have persisted and grown over time.

John moved back to Indianapolis in 2007, and published three novels over the next three years: _[Let It Snow: Three Holiday Romances](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Let_It_Snow:_Three_Holiday_Romances "Let It Snow: Three Holiday Romances")_ (2008, with [Maureen Johnson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maureen_Johnson "Maureen Johnson") and [Lauren Myracle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lauren_Myracle "Lauren Myracle")); his third solo novel, _[Paper Towns](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paper_Towns_(novel) "Paper Towns (novel)")_ (2008); and _[Will Grayson, Will Grayson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Will_Grayson,_Will_Grayson "Will Grayson, Will Grayson")_ (2010, with [David Levithan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Levithan "David Levithan")). From 2010 to 2013, John and Hank launched several online video projects, including [VidCon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/VidCon "VidCon"), an annual conference for the online video community, and _[Crash Course](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crash_Course_(YouTube) "Crash Course (YouTube)")_ (2011–present), a wide-ranging educational channel. Green's 2012 novel, _The Fault in Our Stars_, and [the 2014 film adaptation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Fault_in_Our_Stars_(film) "The Fault in Our Stars (film)") were massive commercial and critical successes, leading to several other film and television adaptations of his work. He was included in _[Time](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time_(magazine) "Time (magazine)")_ magazine's 2014 [list of the 100 most influential people in the world](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time_100 "Time 100").

Green's subsequent projects, his novel _[Turtles All the Way Down](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turtles_All_the_Way_Down_(novel) "Turtles All the Way Down (novel)")_ (2017) and _[The Anthropocene Reviewed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Anthropocene_Reviewed "The Anthropocene Reviewed")_ (2018–2021), dealt more directly with his [anxiety](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anxiety_disorder "Anxiety disorder") and [obsessive–compulsive disorder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Obsessive%E2%80%93compulsive_disorder "Obsessive–compulsive disorder"). _The Anthropocene Reviewed_ began as a podcast in January 2018, with Green reviewing different facets of the [Anthropocene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthropocene "Anthropocene") on a [five-star scale](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Star_(classification) "Star (classification)"). He adapted the podcast into [his first nonfiction book](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Anthropocene_Reviewed:_Essays_on_a_Human-Centered_Planet "The Anthropocene Reviewed: Essays on a Human-Centered Planet") in 2021.

Since the mid-2010s, John Green has been a prominent advocate for [global health](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Global_health "Global health") causes: he is a [trustee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trustee "Trustee") for [Partners In Health](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Partners_In_Health "Partners In Health") (PIH), supporting their goal of reducing [maternal mortality in Sierra Leone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maternal_mortality_in_Sierra_Leone "Maternal mortality in Sierra Leone"), and has worked with PIH and a number of organizations in fighting [tuberculosis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tuberculosis "Tuberculosis") worldwide. Green's second nonfiction book, _[Everything Is Tuberculosis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Everything_Is_Tuberculosis "Everything Is Tuberculosis")_, was released in March 2025. Green's first novel intended for adults, titled _[Hollywood, Ending](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hollywood,_Ending "Hollywood, Ending")_, is set to be released in September 2026.
