# John Green's next book will be nonfiction. Here's what it will be about.

[![Portrait of David Lindquist](https://www.indystar.com/gcdn/-mm-/6e6290854f723372ac779df63518ec0145520e07/c=0-0-256-256/local/-/media/Indianapolis/USATODAY/2014/03/02//1393782930000-0fa6ed7e3548f8d4dca569621e838b41.jpeg?width=48&height=48&fit=crop&format=pjpg&auto=webp) David Lindquist](https://www.indystar.com/staff/2647935001/david-lindquist/)

Indianapolis Star

Updated Aug. 27, 2020, 3:12 p.m. ET

Indianapolis author John Green will set aside stories based on teenagers for his next book and instead will share his opinions on topics ranging from Taco Bell's breakfast menu to the fatal disease cholera.

"The Anthropocene Reviewed," announced today by publisher Dutton, shares its title with Green's monthly podcast in which he critiques things related to humanity's time on Earth – the Anthropocene era – on a five-star scale.
