**Peter Henry Zeihan** ([/ˈzaɪ.ən/](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/English "Help:IPA/English"); born January 18, 1973) is an American author and geopolitical analyst. He is the founder of the consulting firm _Zeihan on Geopolitics_ and a former vice president at the geopolitical intelligence firm [Stratfor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stratfor "Stratfor"). Zeihan has written several books, including _The Accidental Superpower_ (2014), _The Absent Superpower_ (2017), _Disunited Nations_ (2020), and _The End of the World Is Just the Beginning_ (2022).

## Early life and education

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Peter_Zeihan&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Early life and education")\]

Zeihan was born in 1973 and raised in Marshalltown, Iowa, as the adopted son of educators Jerald and Agnes Zeihan. He graduated from Marshalltown High School in 1992.[\[1\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Zeihan#cite_note-1)

He earned a [Bachelor of Science](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bachelor_of_Science "Bachelor of Science") in political science from [Truman State University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Truman_State_University "Truman State University") (then Northeast Missouri State University) in 1995, followed by a postgraduate diploma in Asian studies at the [University of Otago](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Otago "University of Otago") in New Zealand in 1997. Zeihan also attended the University of Kentucky's Patterson School of Diplomacy and International Commerce.[\[2\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Zeihan#cite_note-2)

## Career

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Peter_Zeihan&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Career")\]

After completing his education, Zeihan worked for the American embassy in Australia and at the Center for Political and Strategic Studies, a think tank founded by [Susan Eisenhower](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Susan_Eisenhower "Susan Eisenhower").

In 2000, Zeihan joined the Austin, Texas-based geopolitical intelligence firm [Stratfor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stratfor "Stratfor") as an analyst. Over 12 years, he rose to the position of vice president.[\[3\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Zeihan#cite_note-3)[\[4\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Zeihan#cite_note-4)

He left Stratfor in 2012 to establish _Zeihan on Geopolitics_, a consulting firm serving energy companies, financial institutions, business associations, agricultural organizations, universities, and government entities.[\[5\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Zeihan#cite_note-5)

### Writings

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Peter_Zeihan&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Writings")\]

While at Stratfor, Zeihan co-authored his first book, _A Crucible of Nations_, with Lauren Goodrich in 2011. He also contributed to Stratfor’s 2010 “Decade Forecast,” stating, “we expect the economic collapse of China in this coming decade,” and arguing that structural imbalances would push the system to a “break point” that would shatter the Chinese economic model.[\[6\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Zeihan#cite_note-6)

In 2014, he published _The Accidental Superpower_, which explores the influence of geography on economic, industrial, and military competitiveness. The book emphasizes the strategic importance of navigable rivers, ocean access, transportation infrastructure, and demographic trends.[\[7\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Zeihan#cite_note-7)[\[8\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Zeihan#cite_note-8)[\[9\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Zeihan#cite_note-9)

Subsequent works include _The Absent Superpower_ (2017), _Disunited Nations_ (2020), and _The End of the World Is Just the Beginning_ (2022), each expanding on geopolitical trends, demographic shifts, and the future of globalism.[\[10\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Zeihan#cite_note-10)[\[11\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Zeihan#cite_note-11)

#### Criticism

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Peter_Zeihan&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Criticism")\]

Critics of Zeihan’s work note that he adopts a deterministic view of demographic and geopolitical trends.[\[12\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Zeihan#cite_note-12)[\[13\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Zeihan#cite_note-13)[\[14\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Zeihan#cite_note-14)

Money & Macro criticizes Zeihan's long-running claim, made since 2010, that China will collapse within a decade, noting that while his arguments about demographics, debt, and energy dependence highlight real challenges, many of them are overstated or selective, and the Chinese government has implemented policies to mitigate these risks.[\[15\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Zeihan#cite_note-15)

In the 2023 update of _The Accidental Superpower_, Zeihan appended evaluations of his earlier predictions, acknowledging overestimates of Russia’s military strength and underestimates of Canadian national identity’s influence in Alberta.[\[16\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Zeihan#cite_note-16)

## Personal life

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Peter_Zeihan&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Personal life")\]

In 2001, Zeihan resided in [Austin, Texas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austin,_Texas "Austin, Texas"),[\[17\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Zeihan#cite_note-voteref2001-17) though he now resides in [Morrison, Colorado](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Morrison,_Colorado "Morrison, Colorado").[\[18\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Zeihan#cite_note-18)[\[19\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Zeihan#cite_note-19) He is an [independent voter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Independent_voter "Independent voter").[\[17\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Zeihan#cite_note-voteref2001-17)
