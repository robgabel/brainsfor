Democracy Dies in Darkness

Two-thirds of Americans believe the country is [on the wrong track](https://www.rasmussenreports.com/public_content/politics/mood_of_america/right_direction_or_wrong_track "www.rasmussenreports.com"), a poll number that has not shifted much [in three years](http://elections.huffingtonpost.com/pollster/us-right-direction-wrong-track "elections.huffingtonpost.com"). The [midterm election results](http://www.washingtonpost.com/national/2014/11/05/4156eaf0-5330-11e4-892e-602188e70e9c_story.html "www.washingtonpost.com") were just another reflection of this pervasive discontent. And yet, looking at the rest of the world, what's striking is how
_well_
the United States is doing relative to other major economies. [Japan is back in a recession](http://www.washingtonpost.com/world/japans-economy-tips-back-into-recession-in-another-blow-for-abe/2014/11/16/9a8f2e94-8c9c-44cf-a5e8-b57a470fd61f_story.html "www.washingtonpost.com") and [Germany has barely avoided slipping into one](http://money.cnn.com/2014/09/30/news/economy/germany-recession-risk/ "money.cnn.com"), which would have been its third since 2008. [President Obama says](http://www.whitehouse.gov/the-press-office/2014/11/15/remarks-president-obama-university-queensland "www.whitehouse.gov") the United States has produced more jobs in its recovery [than the rest of the industrialized world](http://www.whitehouse.gov/the-press-office/2014/10/02/remarks-president-economy-northwestern-university "www.whitehouse.gov") put together.
