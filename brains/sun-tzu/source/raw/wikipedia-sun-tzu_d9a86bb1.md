**Sun Tzu** ([/ˌsʊnˈsuː/](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/English "Help:IPA/English"); [traditional Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters "Traditional Chinese characters"):孫子; [simplified Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters "Simplified Chinese characters"):孙子; [pinyin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pinyin "Pinyin"): _Sūnzǐ_) was a Chinese [military general](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General "General"), [strategist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strategist "Strategist"), [philosopher](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philosopher "Philosopher"), and [writer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Writer "Writer") who lived during the [Eastern Zhou period](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Zhou_period "Eastern Zhou period") (771–256 BC). Sun Tzu is traditionally credited as the author of _[The Art of War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Art_of_War "The Art of War")_, a [Classical Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Classical_Chinese "Classical Chinese") text on military strategy from the [Warring States period](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Warring_States_period "Warring States period"), though the earliest parts of the work probably date to at least a century after him.[\[1\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sun_Tzu#cite_note-FOOTNOTELewis1999604-1)

Sun Tzu is revered in [Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_culture "Chinese culture") and East Asian culture as a legendary historical and military figure; however, his historical existence is uncertain. The [Han dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Han_dynasty "Han dynasty") historian [Sima Qian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sima_Qian "Sima Qian") and other traditional Chinese historians placed him as a minister to [King Helü of Wu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/King_Hel%C3%BC_of_Wu "King Helü of Wu") and dated his lifetime to 544–496 BC. The name _Sun Tzu_—by which he is more popularly known—is an [honorific](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_honorifics "Chinese honorifics") which means "Master [Sun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sun_(surname) "Sun (surname)")".
