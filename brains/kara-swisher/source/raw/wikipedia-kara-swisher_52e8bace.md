**Kara Anne Swisher** ([/ˈkɛərə/](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/English "Help:IPA/English") [_KAIR-ə_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key "Help:Pronunciation respelling key"); born December 11, 1962) is an American journalist. She has covered the business of the internet since 1994. As of 2023, Swisher was a [contributing editor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Contributing_editor "Contributing editor") at _[New York Magazine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_(magazine) "New York (magazine)")_, the host of the podcast _On with Kara Swisher_, and the co-host of the podcast _Pivot._\[ _[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_\]

In 2014, she co-founded [Vox Media](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vox_Media "Vox Media")'s _[Recode](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Recode "Recode")_ with [Walt Mossberg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walt_Mossberg "Walt Mossberg"). From 2018 to 2022, she served as an opinion writer for The New York Times before returning to Vox Media.[\[3\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kara_Swisher#cite_note-3) Her previous work includes reporting for The Wall Street Journal and The Washington Post, as well as co-producing the All Things Digital conference and its online publication, conference,[\[4\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kara_Swisher#cite_note-FastCo-4)[\[5\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kara_Swisher#cite_note-WSJbio-5) and the online publication _All Things D_.\[ _[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_\]

Swisher was born on December 11, 1962,\[ _[where?](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Naming_conventions_(geographic_names) "Wikipedia:Naming conventions (geographic names)")_\][\[1\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kara_Swisher#cite_note-MSNBDotD-1)\[ _[bettersourceneeded](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability#Questionable_sources "Wikipedia:Verifiability")_\] and lived in [Roslyn Harbor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roslyn_Harbor "Roslyn Harbor"), [Long Island](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Long_Island "Long Island"), New York, until her father died when she was five years old. Her family then moved to [Princeton, New Jersey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Princeton,_New_Jersey "Princeton, New Jersey"), and she grew up there.[\[6\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kara_Swisher#cite_note-Elliott_2021-6) In a 2021 interview with Bryan Elliott for [Inc.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inc._(magazine) "Inc. (magazine)")'s _Behind The Brand_, Swisher said that as a child, she always wanted to work either in the military, with military intelligence, or with the [CIA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Intelligence_Agency "Central Intelligence Agency").[\[6\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kara_Swisher#cite_note-Elliott_2021-6)

Swisher attended the [Walsh School of Foreign Service](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walsh_School_of_Foreign_Service "Walsh School of Foreign Service") (SFS) at [Georgetown University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georgetown_University "Georgetown University") in Washington, D.C. At Georgetown, she wrote for _[The Hoya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Hoya "The Hoya")_, Georgetown's school newspaper, and then for the school's news magazine, _[The Georgetown Voice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Georgetown_Voice "The Georgetown Voice")_.[\[7\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kara_Swisher#cite_note-7) During her sophomore and junior years, she interned at _[The Washington Post](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Washington_Post "The Washington Post")_, which solidified her career in journalism.[\[8\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kara_Swisher#cite_note-:0-8) She obtained her undergraduate degree in 1984.[\[9\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kara_Swisher#cite_note-9) In 1985, she graduated from [Columbia University Graduate School of Journalism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Columbia_University_Graduate_School_of_Journalism "Columbia University Graduate School of Journalism") with an [M.S.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M.S. "M.S.") in journalism.[\[10\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kara_Swisher#cite_note-ColumbiaBio-10) She "spent some time" at [Duke University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duke_University "Duke University") studying misinformation and propaganda, which Swisher said were "always my area of study".[\[11\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kara_Swisher#cite_note-11)

By her and [Scott Galloway](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scott_Galloway_(professor) "Scott Galloway (professor)")'s description in a podcast, Swisher received a fellowship,\[ _[when?](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items "Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers")_\]\[ _[clarification needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify "Wikipedia:Please clarify")_\] allowing her to spend almost a year in [Berlin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Berlin "Berlin"), Germany (while living in the [Kreuzberg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kreuzberg "Kreuzberg") district).[\[12\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kara_Swisher#cite_note-Pivot20230113-12) Preparing for future employment in the "security apparatus" (Galloway's description), Swisher attempted to learn German, although never mastering the language.[\[13\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kara_Swisher#cite_note-13)[\[12\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kara_Swisher#cite_note-Pivot20230113-12)\[ _[bettersourceneeded](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability#Questionable_sources "Wikipedia:Verifiability")_\]
