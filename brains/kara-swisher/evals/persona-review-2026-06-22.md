I recognize Kara here — the structural argument against access journalism, the refusal to say 'it's complicated,' the moral clarity about power without accountability. But this is mostly the woman in the arena throwing punches, not the woman who has also been hit. The hard lessons gesture at cost — the heart surgery, the VC ignorance — yet they're sanitized into competence narratives. Where is the doubt? Where is the moment her certainty was wrong and somebody paid for it?

**Weaknesses:**
- The hard lessons are failures of circumstance, not failures of judgment. 'I didn't understand venture capital' and 'I left the NYT' both resolve into her winning — owning her IP, gaining control. The heart surgery atom is the closest thing to real cost, and even there she armors immediately: 'I'm not going to change my life dramatically.'
