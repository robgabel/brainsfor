**Oprah Gail Winfrey** ([/ˈoʊprə/](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/English "Help:IPA/English"); born **Orpah Gail Winfrey**;[\[a\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-A_of_A-2) January 29, 1954) is an American [talk show host](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Television_presenter "Television presenter"), television producer, actress, author, and [media proprietor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Media_proprietor "Media proprietor"). She is best known for her talk show, _[The Oprah Winfrey Show](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Oprah_Winfrey_Show "The Oprah Winfrey Show")_, broadcast from [Chicago](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chicago "Chicago"), which ran in national syndication for 25 years, from 1986 to 2011.[\[2\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-2010_contract-3)[\[3\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-Biography-2021-4) Globally, she is the [richest Black woman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_billionaires "Black billionaires")[\[4\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-5) and the [wealthiest female celebrity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_celebrities_by_net_worth "List of celebrities by net worth").[\[5\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-6) Dubbed the " **Queen of All Media**",[\[6\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-7) she was the richest African-American of the 20th century[\[7\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-8)[\[8\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-9) and was once the world's only Black billionaire.[\[9\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-10) By 2007, she was often ranked as the most influential woman in the world.[\[10\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-opens_school-11)[\[11\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-12)

Winfrey was born into poverty in rural [Mississippi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mississippi "Mississippi") to a single teenage mother and later raised in inner-city [Milwaukee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Milwaukee "Milwaukee").[\[12\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-path_to_power-13) Winfrey was then sent to live with the man she calls her father, Vernon Winfrey, a barber in [Nashville, Tennessee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nashville,_Tennessee "Nashville, Tennessee"), and landed a job in radio while still in high school.[\[3\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-Biography-2021-4) By 19, she was a co-anchor for the local evening news. Winfrey's often emotional, extemporaneous delivery eventually led to her transfer to the daytime talk show arena, and after boosting a third-rated local Chicago talk show to first place,[\[13\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-forbes-14) she launched her own production company.

Credited with creating a more intimate, confessional form of media communication,[\[14\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-TIME_100_1998-15) Winfrey popularized and revolutionized[\[14\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-TIME_100_1998-15)[\[15\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-Coming_Afer_Oprah-16) the [tabloid talk show](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tabloid_talk_show "Tabloid talk show") genre pioneered by [Phil Donahue](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phil_Donahue "Phil Donahue").[\[14\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-TIME_100_1998-15) By the mid-1990s, Winfrey had reinvented her show with a focus on literature, self-improvement, mindfulness, and spirituality. She has been criticized for unleashing a confession culture, promoting controversial [self-help](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Self-help "Self-help") ideas,[\[16\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-17) and having an emotion-centered approach,[\[17\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-Chapman2010-18) and has also been praised for overcoming adversity to become a benefactor to others.[\[18\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-TIME_100_2007-19) Winfrey also emerged as a political force in the 2008 presidential race, with [her endorsement of Barack Obama](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey%27s_endorsement_of_Barack_Obama "Oprah Winfrey's endorsement of Barack Obama") estimated to have been worth about one million votes during the [2008 Democratic primaries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2008_Democratic_primaries "2008 Democratic primaries").[\[19\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-20) In the same year, she formed her own network, the [Oprah Winfrey Network](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey_Network "Oprah Winfrey Network") (OWN). In 2013, Winfrey was awarded the [Presidential Medal of Freedom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Presidential_Medal_of_Freedom "Presidential Medal of Freedom") by President [Barack Obama](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barack_Obama "Barack Obama").[\[20\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-21)

In 1994, Winfrey was inducted into the [National Women's Hall of Fame](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Women%27s_Hall_of_Fame "National Women's Hall of Fame").[\[21\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-22) She has received honorary doctorate degrees from multiple universities.[\[22\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-DD-23) Winfrey has won [many awards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_awards_and_nominations_received_by_Oprah_Winfrey "List of awards and nominations received by Oprah Winfrey") throughout her career, including 19 [Daytime Emmy Awards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daytime_Emmy_Award "Daytime Emmy Award") (including the Lifetime Achievement Award and the Chairman's Award), 3 [Primetime Emmy Awards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award "Primetime Emmy Award") (including the [Bob Hope Humanitarian Award](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bob_Hope_Humanitarian_Award "Bob Hope Humanitarian Award")), a [Tony Award](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tony_Award "Tony Award"), a [Peabody Award](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peabody_Award "Peabody Award"), and the [Jean Hersholt Humanitarian Award](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jean_Hersholt_Humanitarian_Award "Jean Hersholt Humanitarian Award") awarded by the [Academy Awards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Academy_Awards "Academy Awards"), in addition to two competitive Academy Award nominations. Winfrey was elected as a member of the [American Academy of Arts and Sciences](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Academy_of_Arts_and_Sciences "American Academy of Arts and Sciences") in 2021.[\[23\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-24)

## Early life

Orpah Gail Winfrey was born on January 29, 1954; her first name was spelled _Orpah_ on her birth certificate after the [biblical figure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orpah "Orpah") in the [Book of Ruth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Book_of_Ruth "Book of Ruth"), but people mispronounced it regularly and "Oprah" stuck.[\[a\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-A_of_A-2) She was born in [Kosciusko, Mississippi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kosciusko,_Mississippi "Kosciusko, Mississippi"), to a teenaged mother,[\[24\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-Observer-2005-25) Vernita Lee, and father Vernon Winfrey. Winfrey's parents never married.[\[25\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-26) Vernita Lee (1935–2018) was a housemaid.[\[26\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-Nelson-1986-27)[\[27\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-28) Vernon Winfrey (1933–2022)[\[28\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-29) was a coal miner turned barber turned city councilman who was in the Armed Forces when she was born.[\[26\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-Nelson-1986-27)[\[b\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-31) A [genetic test](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genetic_test "Genetic test") in 2006 determined that her matrilineal line originated among the [Kpelle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kpelle_people "Kpelle people") ethnic group, from the area that became [Liberia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liberia "Liberia"). Her genetic makeup was determined to be 89% Sub-Saharan African, 8% [Native American](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States#Admixture_and_genetics "Native Americans in the United States")[\[30\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-32)[\[c\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-34), and 3% East Asian.[\[32\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oprah_Winfrey#cite_note-35)
