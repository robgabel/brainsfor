## Ranked fixes
1. **Strip the third-party-opinion and biographical-gossip atoms (Jandali Egypt tour, 'grown-up apartment,' Mueller on patents, Marfatia 'genius who blended') and replace with atoms that model Jobs REASONING — the reality-distortion field demonstrated as a persuasion method, the cannibalization logic applied to a live decision.** — All three judges independently flagged this exact contamination. It's the single highest-leverage fix because it's one problem inflating across rigor, authenticity, and coverage at once — the corpus currently teaches an AI to narrate Jobs rather than think as him.
2. **Add the cruelty and the relational cost as first-class material: 'this is shit' as an operating mode, employees made to cry, the denied paternity of Lisa, friends cut out of options. Frame the reality-distortion field as both inspiration AND self-deception/harm.** — A brain without a shadow is a keynote, not a person. The ruthlessness wasn't incidental to 'A-players only, one B-player is a cancer' — it was the enforcement mechanism. Omitting it makes the operating principles literally non-functional as a model of how he produced quality.
3. **Mark a breakpoint on the integration/closed thesis — at least one atom or hard lesson where 'own the whole widget' or 'closed beats open' demonstrably failed or imposed a real cost.** — Right now the worldview is presented as monotonically validated, which is the motivated-reasoning tell. A brain that only shows its thesis winning can't model the judgment of WHEN the thesis applies — and that conditional is where the actual skill lives.
4. **De-truncate the voice quotes (several cut off mid-word) so claims are verifiable and the voice samples are usable.** — Cheap to fix, and truncated quotes weaken both verifiability and the fidelity of the voice model. Low effort, removes a credibility paper-cut Munger explicitly flagged.

### Rigor / anti-slop — Charlie Munger  ·  78/100

This is largely the real Jobs, not a fortune-cookie impostor. The constitution captures the structural insights that actually drove his fortune — own the whole widget, focus as elimination not ranking, cannibalize yourself, design as how-it-works — and crucially these are stated as causal mechanisms with incentive logic underneath, not slogans. The hard lessons carry receipts with real costs (ousted from his own company, Apple TV price cut from $299 to $229, 70% of the roadmap killed), which is exactly what separates hard-won insight from platitude. My deductions are for atom-level slop: a cluster of biographical filler about his biological father Jandali and third-party opinion atoms that describe Jobs rather than think like him.

**Weaknesses:**
- A meaningful fraction of the atom sample is biographical trivia about Jandali (the Egypt tour scam, the 'grown-up apartment,' 'I didn't trust him not to try to blackmail me') that teaches the AI nothing about how Jobs REASONS — this is gossip wearing the costume of insight
- Several atoms are third parties OPINING ABOUT Jobs rather than Jobs thinking — Florian Mueller on patents, Commissioner Marfatia calling him 'a genius who blended technology and creativity.' That last one is pure platitude; 'blended technology and creativity' is something anyone could say about anyone

**Flags:**

### Coverage of the human / the mess — Brené Brown  ·  62/100
*Recognizable as Steve Jobs: True*

The thinking is here — the focus discipline, the integration conviction, the work-backwards-from-the-customer reversal — and it's rigorous. But you've given me a man without a shadow, and that's not coverage of the human, that's a keynote. The cruelty is missing. The cost he imposed on other people — not the cost the market imposed on him — is almost entirely absent, and without it this is the canonized Jobs, not the real one.

**Weaknesses:**
- The shame and the harm to people is sanitized. 'A-players only; one B-player starts a cancer' is presented as pure operating wisdom — there is no atom acknowledging that this man humiliated people, made employees cry, told them their work was shit. That cruelty IS part of the human and it's been excised
- The hard lessons are all strategic failures (wrong hire, wrong product model). Where is the relational failure? The denied paternity of Lisa. The friends abandoned over options. The reality-distortion field used to manipulate, not inspire. These are the costs he imposed, and they're the truest test of whether the whole human is present
- The 'reality_distortion' atoms treat it as charismatic vision-casting ('do you want to sell sugar water') with no acknowledgment that it was also a tool of denial and steamrolling — the same trait that let him ignore his cancer diagnosis for nine months. That self-destruction is absent entirely
- Doubt is nearly invisible. Every atom radiates conviction. Where is the man who wept, who feared he was a bad father, who reconciled with Lisa late and knew it was late? Conviction without doubt is the highlight reel

**Flags:**
- **The cost to other people — the cruelty, the abandonments — is entirely missing from hard lessons** — _All four hard lessons are strategic ('hired the wrong person,' 'eighteen different directions,' 'wrong mental model'). None is relational or moral._ → Add a hard lesson on the human cost: denying paternity of Lisa, the friends cut out of founder equity, the people he destroyed in meetings — with the receipt being his own later acknowledgment of regret
- **Reality distortion is framed only as inspiration, never as self-deception or harm** — _'This reframe turned a comp...' (Sculley/sugar water) — presented as pure persuasive genius_ → Add an atom capturing the dark side: the same field that let him deny his cancer diagnosis and delay treatment, and that let him deny inconvenient realities to himself
- **No atom on the personal toll or the doubt that would make him real rather than mythic** — _The personal_philosophy atoms only cover the biological father refusal — a clean, justified decision. No mortality reckoning, no parenting regret._ → Add atoms on his late-life reckoning — mortality, the daughter he reached too late — so the brain carries grief and not only conviction
