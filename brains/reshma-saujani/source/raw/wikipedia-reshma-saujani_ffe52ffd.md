**Reshma Saujani** is an American lawyer, activist, and entrepreneur best known as the founder of [Girls Who Code](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Girls_Who_Code "Girls Who Code"), a nonprofit organization focused on reducing the gender gap in technology.

She promotes opportunities for [women in STEM](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Women_in_STEM "Women in STEM"), especially by encouraging young girls to explore computer science from an early age.

Saujani was born in [Chicago, Illinois](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chicago "Chicago"), to Indian immigrant parents who had left [Uganda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uganda "Uganda") due to political unrest. She went on to study at the [University of Illinois](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Illinois "University of Illinois") at Urbana-Champaign before earning a Master’s degree from the [Harvard Kennedy School](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harvard_Kennedy_School "Harvard Kennedy School") and later a law degree from Yale Law School. Early in her career, she worked in law, public service, and finance.\[ _[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_\]

Saujani's idea for Girls Who Code came during her 2010 campaign for the U.S. Congress. While visiting schools, Saujani noticed that computer science classrooms were overwhelmingly male. In response, she founded Girls Who Code in 2012 to provide young women with both the technical skills and the confidence needed to enter the technology industry. Despite the rapid growth of the tech industry, women (especially women of color) have remained underrepresented in fields like [software engineering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software_engineering "Software engineering") and [coding](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_programming "Computer programming"). Saujani often emphasized that girls are socialized to avoid failure, which discourages them from pursuing challenging fields like computer science. Her approach focuses on teaching girls to embrace risk and prioritize learning over perfection. \[ _[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_\]

Saujani's organization, Girls Who Code, has reached hundreds of thousands of students through [summer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Summer "Summer") programs, [clubs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Club_(organization) "Club (organization)"), and partnerships with schools and companies. Many participants have gone on to study computer science in college and pursue careers in technology. Other than Girls Who Code, Saujani has continued her advocacy work through initiatives like the Marshall Plan for Moms, which focuses on supporting working mothers and addressing economic inequalities that became especially visible during the [COVID-19 pandemic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic "COVID-19 pandemic"). She has challenged deeply embedded stereotypes and helped create a more inclusive pathway into technology. \[ _[citation needed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed "Wikipedia:Citation needed")_\]

She worked in city government as a deputy public advocate at the [New York City Public Advocate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_City_Public_Advocate "New York City Public Advocate")'s office.[\[1\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reshma_Saujani#cite_note-1) In 2009, [Saujani ran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2010_United_States_House_of_Representatives_elections_in_New_York#District_14 "2010 United States House of Representatives elections in New York") against [Carolyn Maloney](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carolyn_Maloney "Carolyn Maloney") for the [U.S. House of Representatives](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/U.S._House_of_Representatives "U.S. House of Representatives") seat from [New York's 14th congressional district](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York%27s_14th_congressional_district "New York's 14th congressional district"), becoming the first [Indian American](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_American "Indian American") woman to run for Congress.[\[2\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reshma_Saujani#cite_note-2) In [2013](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2013_New_York_City_Public_Advocate_election "2013 New York City Public Advocate election"), she ran as a [Democratic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States) "Democratic Party (United States)") candidate for Public Advocate, coming third in the primary.[\[3\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reshma_Saujani#cite_note-3)[\[4\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reshma_Saujani#cite_note-4) Following the 2012 founding of Girls Who Code, Saujani was listed in _[Fortune](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fortune_(magazine) "Fortune (magazine)")_’s [40 Under 40](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/40_Under_40 "40 Under 40") list.[\[5\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reshma_Saujani#cite_note-5)

## Early life and education

\[ [edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Reshma_Saujani&action=edit&section=1 "Edit section: Early life and education")\]

Saujani was born in Chicago.[\[6\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reshma_Saujani#cite_note-6) She is of [Gujarati](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gujarati_people "Gujarati people") [Indian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_people "Indian people") descent.[\[7\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reshma_Saujani#cite_note-7)[\[8\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reshma_Saujani#cite_note-8)[\[9\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reshma_Saujani#cite_note-9) Saujani's parents lived in [Uganda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uganda "Uganda"), prior to being [expelled](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Expulsion_of_Asians_from_Uganda "Expulsion of Asians from Uganda") along with other persons of Indian descent in the early 1970s by [Idi Amin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Idi_Amin "Idi Amin").[\[10\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reshma_Saujani#cite_note-HD-10)[\[11\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reshma_Saujani#cite_note-11) They settled in [Chicago](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chicago "Chicago").[\[12\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reshma_Saujani#cite_note-BI-12)

Saujani attended the [University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Illinois_at_Urbana-Champaign "University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign"), where she graduated in 1997 with majors in [Political Science](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_Science "Political Science") and Speech Communication. She attended the [John F. Kennedy School of Government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_F._Kennedy_School_of_Government "John F. Kennedy School of Government") at [Harvard University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harvard_University "Harvard University"), where she received a [Master of Public Policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Master_of_Public_Policy "Master of Public Policy") in 1999, and [Yale Law School](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yale_Law_School "Yale Law School"), where she received her [Juris Doctor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Juris_Doctor "Juris Doctor") in 2002.[\[12\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reshma_Saujani#cite_note-BI-12)
