**Yann André Le Cun**[\[2\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yann_LeCun#cite_note-2) ([/ləˈkʌn/](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/English "Help:IPA/English") [_lə-KUN_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key "Help:Pronunciation respelling key"); French:[\[ləkœ̃\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/French "Help:IPA/French");[\[3\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yann_LeCun#cite_note-auto-3) usually spelled **LeCun**;[\[3\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yann_LeCun#cite_note-auto-3) born 8 July 1960) is a French-American [computer scientist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_scientist "Computer scientist") working in the fields of [artificial intelligence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Artificial_intelligence "Artificial intelligence"), [machine learning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Machine_learning "Machine learning"), [computer vision](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_vision "Computer vision"), [robotics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robotics "Robotics") and [image compression](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Image_compression "Image compression").[\[1\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yann_LeCun#cite_note-gs-1)[\[4\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yann_LeCun#cite_note-deep-4) He is the [Jacob T. Schwartz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jacob_T._Schwartz "Jacob T. Schwartz") Professor of Computer Science at the [Courant Institute of Mathematical Sciences](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Courant_Institute_of_Mathematical_Sciences "Courant Institute of Mathematical Sciences") at [New York University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_University "New York University"). He served as Chief [AI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Artificial_Intelligence "Artificial Intelligence") Scientist at [Meta Platforms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Meta_Platforms "Meta Platforms") before co-founding [Advanced Machine Intelligence Labs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Advanced_Machine_Intelligence_Labs "Advanced Machine Intelligence Labs") in December 2025.[\[5\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yann_LeCun#cite_note-5)[\[6\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yann_LeCun#cite_note-nyt-6)[\[7\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yann_LeCun#cite_note-lunch-7)

He is well known for his work on [optical character recognition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optical_character_recognition "Optical character recognition") and computer vision using [convolutional neural networks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Convolutional_neural_network "Convolutional neural network") (CNNs).[\[4\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yann_LeCun#cite_note-deep-4)[\[8\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yann_LeCun#cite_note-8) He is also one of the main creators of the [DjVu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DjVu "DjVu") image compression technology, alongside [Léon Bottou](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/L%C3%A9on_Bottou "Léon Bottou") and Patrick Haffner. He co-developed the Lush programming language with Léon Bottou.

In 2018, LeCun, [Yoshua Bengio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yoshua_Bengio "Yoshua Bengio"), and [Geoffrey Hinton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geoffrey_Hinton "Geoffrey Hinton") received the [Turing Award](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turing_Award "Turing Award") from the [Association for Computing Machinery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Association_for_Computing_Machinery "Association for Computing Machinery") (ACM) for their work on [deep learning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deep_learning "Deep learning").[\[9\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yann_LeCun#cite_note-9) LeCun, Bengio, and Hinton, and occasionally [Jürgen Schmidhuber](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/J%C3%BCrgen_Schmidhuber "Jürgen Schmidhuber"), are sometimes referred to as the "Godfathers of AI" and "Godfathers of Deep Learning".[\[10\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yann_LeCun#cite_note-10)[\[11\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yann_LeCun#cite_note-11)

## Early life and education

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/Yann_LeCun_at_the_University_of_Minnesota.jpg/960px-Yann_LeCun_at_the_University_of_Minnesota.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Yann_LeCun_at_the_University_of_Minnesota.jpg) LeCun at the [University of Minnesota](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Minnesota "University of Minnesota"), 2014

Yann André Le Cun was born on 8 July 1960 at [Soisy-sous-Montmorency](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soisy-sous-Montmorency "Soisy-sous-Montmorency"), in the suburbs of Paris. His surname, _Le Cun_, derives from the old [Breton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Breton_language "Breton language") form _Le Cunff_ and originates from the region of [Guingamp](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guingamp "Guingamp") in northern [Brittany](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brittany "Brittany"). _[Yann](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yann "Yann")_ is the Breton form of _[Jean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jean_(male_given_name) "Jean (male given name)")_, the French form of _[John](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_(given_name) "John (given name)")_.[\[3\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yann_LeCun#cite_note-auto-3)

He received a _[Diplôme d'Ingénieur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dipl%C3%B4me_d'Ing%C3%A9nieur "Diplôme d'Ingénieur")_ from the [ESIEE Paris](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ESIEE_Paris "ESIEE Paris") in 1983 and a [PhD](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PhD "PhD") in computer science from [Université Pierre et Marie Curie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pierre_and_Marie_Curie_University "Pierre and Marie Curie University") (now [Sorbonne University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sorbonne_University "Sorbonne University")) in 1987, during which he proposed an early form of [backpropagation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Backpropagation "Backpropagation"), an algorithm crucial for enabling neural networks to learn.[\[12\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yann_LeCun#cite_note-12) Before joining AT&T,[\[6\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yann_LeCun#cite_note-nyt-6) LeCun was a [postdoctoral researcher](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Postdoctoral_researcher "Postdoctoral researcher") for a year, starting in 1987, supervised by [Geoffrey Hinton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geoffrey_Hinton "Geoffrey Hinton") at the [University of Toronto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Toronto "University of Toronto").

LeCun has three sons, and his brother is employed by [Google](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google "Google"). He has American citizenship.[\[13\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yann_LeCun#cite_note-13)

## Career and research

LeCun's career has been spent primarily at Bell Labs, New York University and Meta Platforms, Inc.

### Bell Labs

In 1988, LeCun joined the Adaptive Systems Research Department at [AT&T Bell Laboratories](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AT&T_Bell_Laboratories "AT&T Bell Laboratories") in [Holmdel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Holmdel "Holmdel"), New Jersey, United States, headed by Lawrence D. Jackel, where he developed a number of new machine learning methods, such as a biologically inspired model of image recognition called [convolutional neural networks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Convolutional_neural_network "Convolutional neural network") ( [LeNet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/LeNet "LeNet")),[\[14\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yann_LeCun#cite_note-14) the ["Optimal Brain Damage"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Knowledge_distillation#Optimal_Brain_Damage "Knowledge distillation") regularization methods,[\[15\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yann_LeCun#cite_note-15) and the Graph Transformer Networks method (similar to [conditional random field](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conditional_random_field "Conditional random field")), which he applied to handwriting recognition and [Optical character recognition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optical_character_recognition "Optical character recognition") (OCR).[\[16\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yann_LeCun#cite_note-16) The bank check recognition system that he helped develop was widely deployed by [NCR](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NCR_Voyix "NCR Voyix") and other companies.[\[17\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yann_LeCun#cite_note-17)
